# ArayaShiki Lab — Business And Launch

**Version 1.1 | April 2026 | Human-friendly business and launch view | Last synced: 2026-04-09**

---

## What We Are Building

`ArayaShiki Lab` is not positioning itself as a single smart-farm device vendor or a dashboard-only product.

The company direction is a `smart hatchery and aquaculture ecosystem` that connects:

- human operational records
- board and sensor data
- monitoring and alerts
- future automation and process orchestration

Inside that ecosystem, `Daily Complete` is the first flagship operating layer.

It starts with real daily work and structured records, then expands toward device integration and automation over time.

## Market Direction

The company should speak to three market-facing solution tracks:

| Solution track | Primary audience | Core need |
| --- | --- | --- |
| `Research & Education` | institutes, labs, learning centers | structured data collection and repeatable workflows |
| `Farm Operations` | hatcheries and farm operators | practical daily work records, visibility, and monitoring |
| `Enterprise & Custom Automation` | private operators and advanced projects | integrations, permissions, and future process control |

These should be treated as different entry points into the same ecosystem, not as unrelated products.

## Product Framing

### Company

`ArayaShiki Lab` should be presented as the ecosystem builder.

### Flagship Product

`Daily Complete` should be presented as the first production-facing operating layer inside that ecosystem.

### Hardware Path

`SB-00` remains an important pilot and monitoring path, but it should not define the entire company story by itself.

## Packaging Direction

Internally, it is acceptable to continue building a broad feature foundation first.

Externally, the company should package the story clearly so customers can recognize what is relevant to them.

Recommended outward structure:

1. `Research & Education`
2. `Farm Operations`
3. `Enterprise & Custom Automation`

Recommended packaging logic:

- lead with the company ecosystem
- show `Daily Complete` as the flagship solution
- map each audience to the parts of the platform they need first
- keep advanced automation as a growth path, not a day-one promise

## Capability Ladder

The business story should follow a capability ladder instead of pretending every customer buys full automation immediately.

| Layer | What it means commercially |
| --- | --- |
| `Capture` | teams can record work and observations in a structured way |
| `Monitoring` | teams can see state, history, and alerts from people plus devices |
| `Rule-based control` | teams can automate simple responses under clear conditions |
| `Process orchestration` | teams can run multi-step operational workflows with traceable control |

This ladder helps customers understand that the ecosystem can start simple and grow deeper.

## Current Readiness

Current reality:

- monitoring exists at the `SB-00` level
- broader board and actuator expansion is planned
- process automation is a roadmap direction, not a deployed claim

Messaging rule:

- say clearly what is available now
- describe broader automation as planned capability
- avoid implying that closed-loop automation is already production-complete

## Why This Direction Makes Sense

1. the market has many isolated smart-farm tools, but fewer complete operational ecosystems
2. different customer groups have different starting needs
3. building one connected platform reduces fragmentation later
4. the same architecture can support both business and research use cases

## Launch Strategy

### Stage 1 — Prove the operating layer

Focus:

- make `Daily Complete` and the first monitoring workflows real
- validate that manual records and telemetry can live together
- prove that the company story is broader than one pilot device

### Stage 2 — Expand solution fit

Focus:

- show clear value for research teams
- show clear value for hatchery and farm operations
- define what belongs in the enterprise/custom track

### Stage 3 — Expand automation depth

Focus:

- add rule-based automation
- connect more boards and actuators
- prepare for process-level workflows such as controlled water preparation

## Launch Readiness Checklist

Business-side readiness should include:

1. company narrative is clear
2. `Daily Complete` is visible as the flagship solution
3. at least one practical monitoring workflow works end to end
4. solution tracks are understandable by target audiences
5. automation roadmap is ambitious but not overstated

## Decision For Team

The team does not need a long abstract strategy deck before shipping useful work.

What matters now is:

1. make the first operating layer real
2. keep the company story broader than `SB-00`
3. package the same ecosystem differently for each audience
4. expand automation only when monitoring and operational data flow are trustworthy
